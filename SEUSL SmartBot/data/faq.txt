Q: Where is South Eastern University of Sri Lanka (SEUSL) located?
A: SEUSL is located at University Park, Oluvil, #32360, Sri Lanka. The university's permanent campus is in Oluvil, where 220 acres of land has been acquired.

Q: When was SEUSL started?
A: The South Eastern University College of Sri Lanka was established by gazette notification 88/9 of 26 July 1995. It was elevated to a fully-fledged National University on 27th March 1996 and commenced university activities from 15th May 1996.

Q: What are the Faculties in SEUSL and when were they started?
A: SEUSL currently has six faculties:
1. Faculty of Arts and Culture (FAC) - started in 1996 at Addalaichenai, moved to Oluvil in 1998
2. Faculty of Management and Commerce (FMC) - started in 1996 at Addalaichenai, moved to Oluvil in 1998
3. Faculty of Applied Sciences (FAS) - established in 1997 at Sammanthurai
4. Faculty of Islamic Studies & Arabic Language (FIA) - established in October 2005
5. Faculty of Engineering (FE) - established subsequently
6. Faculty of Technology (FT) - established subsequently

Q: What are the Degree Programmes offered by each Faculty?
A:

- FMC (Management and Commerce): Degrees in Accountancy, Finance, Management, IT, Marketing Management
- FAS (Applied Sciences): B.Sc. in Biological Sciences, Physical Sciences, Computer Science, Mathematical Sciences, Chemical Sciences

Q: What is the qualification needed to enter SEUSL?
A: Admission to undergraduate programs at SEUSL is through the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka based on results from the G.C.E. Advanced Level (A/L) examination. Students are admitted based on their A/L results and the UGC's national admissions policy.

Q: How can I apply to become a student of SEUSL?
A: Undergraduate admissions are handled by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka. Students apply through UGC after receiving their A/L results. For postgraduate programs, applications are submitted directly to the respective faculty's postgraduate unit. Contact the Registrar's office for more information: registrar@seu.ac.lk or +94 67 2255168.

Q: What is the Medium of Instruction at SEUSL?
A: The medium of instruction varies by faculty. Generally, English is used in the Faculty of Applied Sciences, Faculty of Engineering, and Faculty of Technology. Tamil and English are used in the Faculty of Arts and Culture and Faculty of Management and Commerce. Arabic is used in the Faculty of Islamic Studies and Arabic Language.

Q: Do I need to be fluent in English to follow courses at SEUSL?
A: A good working knowledge of English is helpful, especially for faculties where English is the medium of instruction (FAS, FE, FT). SEUSL has an English Language Teaching Unit (ELTU) to support students with English language skills.

Q: What is the course structure of the degree programmes offered at SEUSL?
A: Degree programmes at SEUSL are structured on a credit-based system with semesters. Students must complete a required number of credits through a combination of core courses, electives, and practical work to be eligible for their degree.

Q: What is End Semester Examination (ESE)?
A: End Semester Examinations are formal written examinations held at the end of each semester. They form a major component of student assessment for each course unit.

Q: What is Continues Assessment (CA)?
A: Continuous Assessment (CA) refers to ongoing evaluation throughout the semester through assignments, practicals, quizzes, mid-semester tests, presentations, and other in-course activities. CA results are combined with ESE results to determine final grades.

These are offered through the Postgraduate Units of the respective faculties (FMC, FAC, FT).
