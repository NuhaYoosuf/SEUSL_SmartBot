CHUNK_ID: KB-POL-001
TOPIC: Institutional Repository Policy (SEUIR)
CATEGORY: policies
---
SEUSL Institutional Repository (SEUIR) — established in 2006. Website: http://ir.lib.seu.ac.lk/
Software: Initially built on GreenStone Digital Library (GSDL); migrated to DSpace 4.0 in 2014; upgraded to DSpace 5.5 in November 2015.
Mission: To be a nodal point of access for scholarship and creative work contributed by the university, expanding the visibility of university intellectual scholarship to the regional and global community.

Accepted content: Conference proceedings of SEUSL, Theses and dissertations (undergraduate, postgraduate, and academic), Journals published by SEUSL, Articles authored by SEUSL academics (with copyright clearance), Books and book chapters (non-textbooks, case-by-case), Data sets (case-by-case), Grey literature (technical reports, working papers, annual reports — case-by-case).

Submission rules: Only SEUSL members may submit. Only the SEUIR administrator or authorized person can deposit valid content. Academics can upload articles to the "Research Articles" collection. Authors are fully responsible for content validity and authenticity. Plagiarized content is the sole responsibility of the author. If the author does not own copyright, publisher permission must be obtained. University publications must be submitted within one calendar year.

Accessibility: Items freely accessible via http://ir.lib.seu.ac.lk/. Articles are indexed in Google and Google Scholar. Creative Commons licenses supported.

Retention: Scholarly materials retained permanently unless they infringe copyright, contain falsified research, or fail legal requirements.

Withdrawal: Acceptable reasons include copyright violation, legal requirement, national security, falsified research, or duplicates. Authors must submit a prescribed form with editor/coordinator recommendation. The Librarian has sole authority over the Digital Library.

CHUNK_ID: KB-POL-002
TOPIC: Attendance Policy
CATEGORY: policies
---
SEUSL Attendance Policy: A minimum of 80% attendance is required for each course unit to be eligible to sit the end semester examination. Students who fall below 80% attendance in a course receive a 'not eligible' notation and must repeat that course. Attendance regulations are available for download at https://www.seu.ac.lk/guidelines.php

CHUNK_ID: KB-POL-003
TOPIC: Academic Integrity and Plagiarism Policy
CATEGORY: policies
---
SEUSL Academic Integrity Policy: SEUSL has a strict academic integrity policy. Plagiarism in assignments can result in a zero mark for that assessment, course failure, or suspension from the university. All written assignments are subject to plagiarism checks. Students are required to use proper citation and referencing in all academic work. Turnitin plagiarism detection software is available through the university library.
