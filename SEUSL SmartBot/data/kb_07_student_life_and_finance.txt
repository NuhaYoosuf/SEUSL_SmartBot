CHUNK_ID: KB-HOSTEL-001
TOPIC: Hostel Accommodation
CATEGORY: hostel
---
SEUSL provides on-campus hostel accommodation for both male and female students. Sub-Warden posts exist for both male and female hostels. Spaces are limited — priority is given to students who live far from the university, students from low-income families, and first-year students. For accommodation information, contact the university administration, welfare office, or SSSW.
Student Accommodations Division: http://www.seu.ac.lk/generaladmin/stacc/

Hostel Room Facilities: Basic rooms have a bed, study table, chair, and wardrobe/shelf space. Common facilities include shared bathrooms, toilets, a common room/TV room, and a study hall. Students must bring their own bedding, pillow, towel, and study materials.

Hostel Rules: Curfew for female hostels is usually 10 PM. Rules include no unauthorized visitors in rooms, no cooking inside rooms, no loud music after 10 PM, maintain cleanliness of common areas.

CHUNK_ID: KB-HOSTEL-002
TOPIC: Off-Campus Accommodation
CATEGORY: hostel
---
Off-campus accommodation options near SEUSL (Oluvil):
- Basic room: approximately LKR 3,000–6,000/month
- Room with attached bathroom and internet: approximately LKR 6,000–10,000/month
- Sharing with another student reduces costs significantly
Senior students and the university welfare office can help find boarding houses near campus. Kalmunai (5 km north) has more accommodation options.

CHUNK_ID: KB-FIN-001
TOPIC: University Fees and Free Education
CATEGORY: finance
---
Sri Lanka provides free university education at SEUSL for students admitted through the UGC system — no tuition fees are charged for undergraduate degrees. However, students are required to pay small administrative fees:
- Registration fee (one-time at the start of the degree)
- Examination fees per semester
- Library fees
- Hostel fees (if residing in university hostel)
For financial clearance at graduation, visit the Bursar's office in the main administration building.

CHUNK_ID: KB-FIN-002
TOPIC: Mahapola Scholarship
CATEGORY: finance
---
Mahapola Higher Education Scholarship:
- Provides a monthly allowance of approximately LKR 5,000–7,500 to eligible undergraduates at state universities
- Eligibility based on academic merit and financial need
- Application: Apply through your faculty's student affairs office during the first month of registration
- This is a national scholarship scheme for Sri Lankan university students

CHUNK_ID: KB-FIN-003
TOPIC: Bursary Financial Grant
CATEGORY: finance
---
Bursary Financial Grant:
- A needs-based financial grant from the university for students with proven financial hardship
- Separate from the Mahapola scholarship; provides additional funds for living expenses, books, etc.
- Application: Submit with an income declaration signed by your Grama Niladhari (GN officer)
- Apply through the university's welfare office or student affairs division

CHUNK_ID: KB-FIN-004
TOPIC: Cost of Living in Oluvil
CATEGORY: finance
---
Estimated monthly student budget in Oluvil (one of the most affordable university towns in Sri Lanka):
- Accommodation (off-campus): LKR 3,000–8,000
- Food: LKR 6,000–10,000
- Transport: LKR 1,000–2,000
- Stationery and books: LKR 500–2,000
- Personal expenses: LKR 1,000–3,000
- Total: approximately LKR 12,000–25,000 per month
University hostel and subsidized canteen food significantly reduce costs.

CHUNK_ID: KB-LIFE-001
TOPIC: Orientation Week
CATEGORY: student_life
---
Freshers' orientation week at SEUSL:
Activities include: campus and faculty tour, Dean and Head of Department welcome address, course registration, hostel room assignment, anti-ragging briefing (mandatory under UGC law), student union and society introductions, and senior student mentoring sessions. Attendance at all orientation activities is compulsory.
Freshers' Night: Celebratory event organized by second-year students a few weeks after orientation. Features cultural performances, music, welcome speeches, crowning of Freshers' King/Queen, and student skits.

CHUNK_ID: KB-LIFE-003
TOPIC: Campus Environment and Safety
CATEGORY: student_life
---
Oluvil is a small, quiet coastal community — the university campus is its main landmark. The area is generally very safe for students. The campus is predominantly Muslim-friendly: prayer facilities are on campus, the canteen serves halal food, and the academic calendar respects Islamic holidays. Non-Muslim students are fully welcomed; there is a diverse student community. Tamil is the most widely spoken language on and around campus. Basic Tamil phrases are helpful in the community. The campus beach is a short walk away.

CHUNK_ID: KB-LIFE-004
TOPIC: Industrial Training and Internships
CATEGORY: career
---
Industrial training at SEUSL:
- Typically takes place in Year 3 or between Year 3 and Year 4
- Duration: usually 6 months to 1 year
- The university provides a list of approved companies; students can also find their own placement subject to university approval
- Coordinated by the Head of Department and the Career Guidance Unit
- Start searching at least 3–4 months before the IT period begins
The Faculty of Applied Sciences has a dedicated Industrial Training Cell (ITC) established in 2018.
