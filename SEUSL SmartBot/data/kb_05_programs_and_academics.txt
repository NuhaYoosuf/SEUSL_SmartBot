CHUNK_ID: KB-PROG-001
TOPIC: Admissions Process
CATEGORY: admissions
---
Undergraduate admissions to SEUSL are handled exclusively by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka. Students must apply through UGC after receiving G.C.E. Advanced Level (A/L) examination results. Admission is based on A/L results, Z-score calculations, district quotas, and the UGC's national admissions policy. A/L results and Z-score calculations are typically available around May–July each year. Admission letters are sent by UGC followed by SEUSL's Registrar's office. Registration and orientation usually takes place between October and January. International students can contact the OSPIM office: http://www.seu.ac.lk/ospim/index.php. Postgraduate program applications are submitted directly to the respective faculty's postgraduate unit.

CHUNK_ID: KB-PROG-010
TOPIC: Academic Structure and Credits
CATEGORY: academics
---
SEUSL uses a credit-based semester system. Key academic structure details:
- Minimum credits to graduate: 120–130 credits
- Each course carries 2–4 credits; one credit = one hour of lecture per week per semester
- Students typically take 5–7 courses per semester (15–21 credits per semester)
- GPA scale: 4.0 system. Grades: A+/A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, E (Fail) = 0.0
- Honors classifications: First Class = CGPA 3.7+, Second Class Upper = CGPA 3.3+, Second Class Lower = CGPA 2.7+
- Minimum CGPA to progress to next year: 2.0
- Academic probation: issued when CGPA falls below 2.0. Two consecutive semesters below threshold may result in withdrawal from program.
- Minimum attendance: 80% per course to sit final examination. Students below 80% receive 'not eligible' notation and must repeat the course.

CHUNK_ID: KB-PROG-011
TOPIC: Examinations and Assessment
CATEGORY: examinations
---
Examination and assessment system at SEUSL:
- End Semester Examination (ESE): Formal written examinations held at the end of each semester. Major component of student assessment.
- Continuous Assessment (CA): Ongoing evaluation throughout the semester via assignments, practicals, quizzes, mid-semester tests, presentations, and in-course activities. CA combined with ESE determines final grades.
- Typical assessment split: 40% CA + 60% ESE final examination. Some courses are 100% coursework or 100% exam — check the course outline given at the start of each semester.
- Minimum passing grade: D (approximately 40% of total marks). Below D is classified as E (Fail). For some postgraduate/specialized courses, C (50%) is the minimum pass.
- Guidelines for examinations: https://www.seu.ac.lk/guidelines.php

CHUNK_ID: KB-PROG-012
TOPIC: Exam Policies — Illness, Plagiarism, Academic Integrity
CATEGORY: examinations
---
Examination policies at SEUSL:
- Missed exam due to illness: Submit a medical certificate from a government hospital to the Registrar/Examinations Division within 5 working days. Apply for a medical repeat examination. Approval is at the discretion of the Faculty Examination Board. Private clinic certificates may not be accepted.
- Academic dishonesty/plagiarism: SEUSL has a strict academic integrity policy. Plagiarism can result in a zero mark for that assessment, course failure, or suspension. All written assignments are subject to plagiarism checks (Turnitin available via university library). Use proper citation and referencing.
- Regulations available for download: Guidelines to Supervisors and Invigilators, Regulations Related to Maintaining Attendance and Calculation of Percentage Attendance, Regulations Related to Setting, Printing and Evaluation of Examinations — all at https://www.seu.ac.lk/guidelines.php

CHUNK_ID: KB-PROG-013
TOPIC: Graduation Requirements
CATEGORY: academics
---
Requirements to graduate from SEUSL:
1. Complete all compulsory courses in your degree program
2. Accumulate minimum 120–130 total credits
3. Maintain minimum CGPA of 2.0 throughout
4. Complete industrial training successfully (for applicable programs)
5. Submit and defend your final year research project
6. Clear all library dues and hostel dues
7. Obtain financial clearance from the Bursar's office

After graduation: Apply to the Registrar's office for certified copies of your degree certificate and academic transcript. Submit written request, NIC, and pay the applicable fee. Allow 1–2 weeks for processing.
