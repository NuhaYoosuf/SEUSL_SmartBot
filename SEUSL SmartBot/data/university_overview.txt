HISTORY:
The South Eastern University College of Sri Lanka was established by gazette notification 88/9 of 26 July 1995 under the provisions of section 24A of the University Act No. 16 of 1978. It was established to accommodate Muslim staff and students displaced from the University of Jaffna and later the Eastern University during civil unrest in the North and East of Sri Lanka.

The University College was ceremonially inaugurated with a batch of 33 students in October 1995. By gazette notification 916/7 of 27th March 1996, it was elevated to a fully-fledged National University under the corporate name of South Eastern University of Sri Lanka. It commenced its full university activities from 15th May 1996.

The University started with two Faculties — Faculty of Arts & Culture and Faculty of Management and Commerce — at Addalaichenai, sharing the premises of the Government Teachers' Training College. The permanent campus is located at Oluvil where 220 acres of land was acquired.

In 1997, the Faculty of Applied Sciences was established at Sammanthurai. In May 1998, Faculty of Arts & Culture and Faculty of Management & Commerce moved to the permanent seat at Oluvil. In October 2005, the Faculty of Islamic Studies and Arabic Language was established — the only faculty of its kind in any Sri Lankan university.
