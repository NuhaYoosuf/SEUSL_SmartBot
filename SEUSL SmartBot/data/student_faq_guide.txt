SECTION 1: GENERAL & FIRST ARRIVAL
==============================================================

Q: What is SEUSL and where exactly is it located?
A: South Eastern University of Sri Lanka (SEUSL) is a national university established in 2005 and fully recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka. It is located in Oluvil, in the Ampara District of the Eastern Province — about 320 km from Colombo along the southern coastal road. The campus sits near the Indian Ocean coastline.

Q: How do I get to SEUSL from Colombo?
A: The most common route is by bus from Colombo Central Bus Stand (Bastian Mawatha) — intercity buses to Ampara or Kalmunai pass through Oluvil. The journey takes approximately 7–9 hours. Alternatively, take a train to Batticaloa and then a bus south to Oluvil (~1.5 hours). Some students hire vans/cabs for the journey, especially on registration day with luggage.

Q: What are the nearest towns to the campus?
A: The nearest significant town is Kalmunai (about 5 km north), which has banks, supermarkets, pharmacies, and shopping facilities. Ampara town (the district capital) is about 40 km away. Batticaloa is approximately 70 km north and has a larger city with more facilities.

Q: Is Oluvil a safe place to live as a student?
A: Yes. Oluvil is a small, quiet coastal community and the university campus is its main landmark. The area is generally very safe for students. The town has improved significantly in infrastructure since the post-war development period. Female students in particular benefit from the campus hostel system.

Q: What language is mostly spoken on campus?
A: SEUSL is predominantly a Tamil-medium environment, as it is located in the Eastern Province. Tamil is the most widely spoken language on and around campus. However, Sinhala-speaking students are also present, and English is used as the medium of instruction for most undergraduate programs. Basic Tamil phrases will help you in the community.

Q: Is SEUSL a state university recognized by the UGC?
A: Yes. SEUSL is fully recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Sri Lanka and is a national state university. Degrees awarded by SEUSL are recognized by the government for public sector employment and are internationally recognized for postgraduate studies abroad.

Q: When does the academic year start for new students?
A: There is no single fixed date — it follows the national UGC admission cycle. After A/L results and Z-score calculations (typically around May–July), UGC sends admission letters. Registration and orientation at SEUSL usually takes place between October and January. Your specific registration date will be stated in your official admission letter from SEUSL's Registrar's office. Monitor seu.ac.lk regularly.

Q: What is freshers' orientation week and what happens there?
A: Orientation week is your first official week on campus. Activities include: campus and faculty tour, Dean and HoD welcome address, course registration, hostel room assignment, anti-ragging briefing (mandatory by UGC law), student union and society introductions, and senior student mentoring sessions. It is compulsory to attend all orientation activities.

Q: Who is the current Vice-Chancellor of SEUSL?
A: The Vice-Chancellor is the head of the university appointed by the University Council. Please check the official SEUSL website (seu.ac.lk) for the current VC's name, as appointments change periodically. The VC's office is located in the main administration building on campus.

Q: What are the six faculties in SEUSL?
A: SEUSL has six faculties: (1) Faculty of Arts & Culture, (2) Faculty of Management & Commerce, (3) Faculty of Technology, (4) Faculty of Applied Sciences, (5) Faculty of Islamic Studies & Arabic Language, and (6) Faculty of Engineering. Each faculty has its own Dean, departments, and programs.

Q: Is SEUSL a Muslim-majority university environment?
A: SEUSL is located in a predominantly Muslim area and many students and staff are Muslim. The campus environment is respectful of Islamic practices — there are prayer facilities on campus, the canteen serves halal food, and the academic calendar respects Islamic holidays. Non-Muslim students are fully welcomed and there is a diverse student community.

Q: What is the overall campus size and environment like?
A: The campus spans a substantial area (220 acres) near the southeastern coastline. It has a green, open layout with separate zones for faculties, administration, hostels, sports grounds, and student facilities. The beach is only a short walk from campus. The environment is calm and conducive to study, though remote — plan your visits to Kalmunai or Colombo in advance.

SECTION 2: ADMINISTRATION & REGISTRATION
==============================================================

Q: How do I officially register as a new student?
A: On your designated registration day (stated in your admission letter), report to your faculty's registration desk with all required documents. You will fill in registration forms, pay any required fees, receive your student index number, and be assigned to your department. The process typically takes one full day.

Q: What documents do I need to bring on registration day?
A: Bring originals and photocopies of: National Identity Card (NIC), A/L exam results certificate, O/L exam results certificate, birth certificate, medical fitness certificate (from a government hospital), police clearance certificate, 6–8 passport-size photographs, school leaving certificate, and the UGC admission letter. Keep extra photocopies of everything.

Q: Where is the Registrar's office and what are its working hours?
A: The Registrar's office is located in the main administration building at the center of the SEUSL campus. Working hours are generally Monday to Friday, 8:30 AM to 4:15 PM (Sri Lanka government office hours). It is closed on public holidays and Poya days.

Q: How do I get my student ID card?
A: Student ID cards are issued within the first few weeks of registration. You will be photographed on campus or submit a photo during registration. The card is issued by the Registrar's office. Keep it always — you need it for library access, exams, and leaving the hostel.

Q: What is my student index number and how do I use it?
A: Your student index number is your unique university identifier (e.g., CS/2024/001). It is used on all exam answer sheets, official letters, LMS login, library registration, and fee payments. Memorize it from day one — never write your name instead of your index number on exams.

Q: Who is my Academic Advisor and how do I contact them?
A: Each student is assigned a personal Academic Advisor — a senior lecturer in your department. Their details are given during orientation. They help with course selection, academic difficulties, and guidance. Reach out via university email or visit their staff room during their designated consultation hours.

Q: Where do I go if I have an administrative problem?
A: For academic issues: your Head of Department (HoD). For financial matters: the Bursar's office. For official letters/records: the Registrar's office. For hostel issues: the Warden. For welfare problems: the Dean of Student Affairs. For urgent personal issues: the Student Counseling Unit.

Q: How do I update my personal information in university records?
A: Submit a written request to the Registrar's office with your index number, the information to be changed, and supporting documents (e.g., new NIC for address change). Some updates can also be made through the university's online student portal if available.

Q: What is the process to defer or suspend my studies?
A: Submit a formal written application to the Registrar via your HoD with valid reasons (medical, family emergency, etc.) and supporting documents. Medical deferral requires a certified government hospital letter. The University Council approves deferments. A maximum of one academic year deferment is usually allowed during the full degree program.

Q: How do I apply for a student confirmation letter?
A: Submit a written request or online form to the Registrar's office specifying the purpose (bank account, scholarship, visa, etc.). The letter is usually issued within 3–5 working days. A small fee may apply. Bring your student ID when collecting it.

Q: Who is the Registrar of SEUSL?
A: The Registrar is the chief administrative officer of the university, responsible for all academic records, examinations administration, and official documentation. For the current Registrar's name, visit seu.ac.lk/administration or contact the main university office at +94 67 2255 071.

Q: What is the role of the Bursar's office?
A: The Bursar's office handles all financial matters of the university including student fee collections, scholarship disbursements, staff salaries, and university accounts. If you have questions about Mahapola/Bursary payments, fee receipts, or financial clearances, this is the office to visit.

SECTION 3: ACADEMIC & COURSES
==============================================================

Q: What degree program am I enrolled in and what is the full course structure?
A: Your degree program is a 4-year Bachelor's degree (8 semesters). Each semester lasts approximately 15–18 weeks. Your program has a set of core/compulsory courses and a number of electives. A detailed course structure booklet (curriculum handbook) is distributed during orientation or available from your department office.

Q: How is the credit system at SEUSL structured?
A: SEUSL uses a credit-based system. Each course carries a specific number of credits (usually 2–4 credits per course). One credit typically corresponds to one hour of lecture per week per semester. You need to complete a minimum total number of credits (usually 120–130 credits) to graduate.

Q: What is the difference between compulsory and elective courses?
A: Compulsory (core) courses are mandatory for your degree — all students in your program must take them. Elective courses allow you to choose from a list of approved subjects based on your interests or specialization. The number of electives varies by program — your curriculum guide will list them clearly.

Q: How do I add or drop a course after registration?
A: Course add/drop is allowed only during the first 2 weeks of each semester (the add/drop period). Submit a course change form to your department. Some courses require HoD approval. After the add/drop period, you cannot change courses without formal academic committee approval.

Q: What is the GPA system used at SEUSL?
A: SEUSL uses a 4.0 GPA scale. Grade boundaries: A+ = 4.0, A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, E (Fail) = 0.0. Your cumulative GPA (CGPA) is calculated at the end of each semester across all courses.

Q: What GPA do I need to maintain to pass each year?
A: A minimum CGPA of 2.0 is typically required to progress to the next year of study. Graduating with honors requires: Second Class Lower = CGPA 2.7+, Second Class Upper = CGPA 3.3+, First Class = CGPA 3.7+. Confirm exact thresholds with your faculty's examination regulations.

Q: What happens if I fail a course — can I repeat it?
A: Yes. If you fail a course (grade E), you must repeat it in the next available semester it is offered. Some courses have supplementary exams. Repeated courses may affect your GPA calculation depending on the policy — in some schemes, only the latest grade counts; in others, both appear on your transcript.

Q: How many courses do I take per semester?
A: Typically 5–7 courses per semester, amounting to 15–21 credits. The exact number depends on your program and year. First-year students usually take a fixed set of courses with little choice. Electives increase from the second year onwards.

Q: Are there any English language requirements or foundation courses?
A: Most programs at SEUSL are conducted in English. If your English proficiency needs improvement, foundation English courses may be offered. Some faculties have English Communication Skills as a compulsory first-year course. There is no separate IELTS/TOEFL requirement for domestic students.

Q: What is the medium of instruction at SEUSL?
A: English is the medium of instruction for most undergraduate programs, particularly in Technology, Applied Sciences, and Management faculties. The Faculty of Arts & Culture and Faculty of Islamic Studies offer some programs in Tamil or Arabic. Check your specific program's medium in the curriculum handbook.

Q: How do I access my timetable?
A: Timetables are posted on the department notice board at the start of each semester. Many departments also share timetables via WhatsApp groups, the LMS (Learning Management System), or email. Save your timetable photo on your phone on day one.

Q: Are there any industrial training or internship requirements?
A: Yes — most programs (especially Technology, Management, and Applied Sciences) require a compulsory industrial training (IT) period, usually in Year 3 or between Year 3 and Year 4. Duration is typically 6 months to 1 year. You must find a placement with a registered company, and the university coordinates and monitors it.

Q: What is the final year research/thesis requirement?
A: In Year 4, all students complete a research project or thesis as a major component of their degree. You select a topic with a supervisor (a faculty member), conduct original research, submit a written dissertation, and present/defend it before an examination panel. This usually carries 6–12 credits.

Q: How do I choose my specialization in later years?
A: Specialization selection usually occurs at the end of Year 2 or beginning of Year 3. You indicate your preferences, and placements are made based on academic performance (GPA) and available slots. Some programs have a single specialization track; others offer 2–3 streams. Your Academic Advisor will guide you.

Q: What postgraduate programs does SEUSL offer?
A: SEUSL offers postgraduate programs including Master's degrees (MA, MSc, MBA, MPhil) and PhD programs in various disciplines. Programs are offered by the Postgraduate Studies and Research Division. Eligibility typically requires a relevant Bachelor's degree with a minimum GPA. Check seu.ac.lk/postgraduate for current offerings.

SECTION 4: EXAMINATIONS & ASSESSMENTS
==============================================================

Q: How is the assessment structured — exams vs coursework?
A: Assessment is split between coursework (continuous assessment) and end-of-semester examinations. A typical split is 40% coursework (assignments, mid-semester tests, practicals, presentations) and 60% final examination. Some courses are 100% coursework or 100% exam — check the course outline distributed at the start of each semester.

Q: When are semester exams usually held?
A: End-of-semester examinations are held in approximately December–January (for Semester 1) and May–June (for Semester 2). Exact dates are announced by the Examinations Division 4–6 weeks in advance. Always check the official timetable — do not rely on estimates.

Q: Where can I find the exam timetable?
A: Exam timetables are published on the SEUSL official website (seu.ac.lk), the department notice board, the LMS, and faculty WhatsApp groups. The Examinations Division office also displays the timetable. Download and save it as soon as it is released.

Q: What is the minimum attendance required to sit for exams?
A: A minimum of 80% attendance is required for each course to be eligible to sit the final examination. If your attendance falls below 80% due to illness or valid reasons, you must apply to your HoD with supporting documents. Students denied exam eligibility due to attendance receive a 'not eligible' notation and must repeat the course.

Q: What happens if I miss an exam due to illness?
A: If you miss an exam due to a serious medical condition, submit a medical certificate from a government hospital to the Registrar/Examinations Division within 5 working days. Apply for a medical repeat examination. Approval is at the discretion of the Faculty Examination Board. Private clinic certificates may not be accepted — always use a government hospital.

Q: How do I apply for a medical certificate-based exemption?
A: Obtain an official medical certificate from a government hospital doctor. Submit it with a written application to your HoD and the Examinations Division within 5 working days of the missed exam. Include your index number, course code, date of exam missed, and the medical certificate. Keep a copy for yourself.

Q: How are exam results published and when?
A: Results are published on the SEUSL student portal (accessible via the university website) approximately 6–10 weeks after exams. Results are also displayed on faculty notice boards. You will receive a formal result sheet from the Examinations Division for your records.

Q: What is the procedure to appeal or re-check an exam result?
A: If you believe a result is incorrect, submit a formal re-scrutiny/re-marking application to the Examinations Division within 2 weeks of results being published. Pay the re-scrutiny fee. The paper is re-checked by a second examiner. Results of re-scrutiny are final and binding.

Q: What is the passing grade for a course at SEUSL?
A: The minimum passing grade is D (which corresponds to approximately 40% of the total marks). A grade below D is classified as E (Fail). For some postgraduate or specialized courses, a C (50%) may be the minimum pass. Check your specific program's examination regulations.

Q: What is academic probation and when does it apply?
A: Academic probation is issued when your CGPA falls below the minimum threshold (usually 2.0) at the end of a semester. A probation warning is sent and you must improve your CGPA in the following semester. If your CGPA remains below the threshold for two consecutive semesters, you may be required to withdraw from the program.

Q: Are there supplementary or repeat exams?
A: Yes. Many programs offer supplementary exams for students who narrowly fail (e.g., grade D-). These are held approximately 4–6 weeks after the main exam season. Not all courses or programs have supplementary exams — confirm with your department. Students who fail the supplementary must repeat the full course in the next available semester.

Q: What is the policy on academic dishonesty and plagiarism?
A: SEUSL has a strict academic integrity policy. Plagiarism (copying from books, internet, or other students without attribution) can result in a zero mark for that assessment, course failure, or in serious cases, suspension. All written assignments are subject to plagiarism checks. Use proper citation and referencing in all academic work.

Q: How do I request my official academic transcript?
A: Submit a written request to the Registrar's office with your student index number, full name, degree program, and purpose of transcript. Transcripts are usually ready within 5–10 working days. A fee is charged per copy. Official transcripts are sealed and stamped — do not open sealed transcripts intended for institutions.

Q: What is a degree certificate and when is it issued after graduation?
A: The degree certificate is the official document confirming your qualification. It is issued approximately 6–12 months after your final examination results are confirmed and all clearances are completed (library, hostel, financial). An official graduation ceremony (convocation) is held annually where certificates are awarded in person.

SECTION 5: HOSTEL & ACCOMMODATION
==============================================================

Q: Does SEUSL provide student hostel accommodation?
A: Yes. SEUSL provides on-campus hostel accommodation for both male and female students. However, spaces are limited and priority is given to students who live far from the university, students from low-income families, and first-year students. Apply as early as possible — ideally during registration.

Q: How do I apply for a hostel room?
A: Submit a hostel application form (available from the Warden's office or student affairs office) during or shortly after registration. Attach proof of distance from home (GN certificate) and income details if applicable. Rooms are allocated based on availability and priority criteria.

Q: Is accommodation guaranteed for first-year students?
A: Accommodation is not automatically guaranteed, but first-year students are generally given priority. Given SEUSL's remote location, the university makes efforts to accommodate as many first-year students as possible. If hostel space is unavailable, you must arrange off-campus boarding in Oluvil or nearby Kalmunai.

Q: What is the hostel fee and how is it paid?
A: Hostel fees are charged per semester and are subsidized for state university students. The exact fee varies by hostel type and room sharing (single, double, or dormitory). Fees are paid to the Bursar's office at the beginning of each semester. Keep all payment receipts safely.

Q: Are there separate hostels for male and female students?
A: Yes. SEUSL has separate hostel blocks for male and female students with different wardens. Entry between blocks is strictly prohibited. Female hostels typically have additional security measures and earlier curfew timings.

Q: What facilities are inside the hostel rooms?
A: Basic hostel rooms have a bed, study table, chair, and wardrobe/shelf space. Common facilities include shared bathrooms, toilets, a common room/TV room, and a study hall. Some newer blocks may have better facilities. Bring your own bedding, pillow, towel, and study materials.

Q: What are the hostel rules and curfew timings?
A: Common hostel rules: return by the curfew time (usually 10 PM for female hostels, later or open for male hostels — check your specific hostel rules), no unauthorized visitors in rooms, no cooking inside rooms (fire hazard), no loud music after 10 PM, maintain cleanliness of common areas, and report maintenance issues to the warden.

Q: Is Wi-Fi available in the hostels?
A: Wi-Fi availability in hostels varies. The university's main campus has Wi-Fi in lecture halls and libraries. Hostel Wi-Fi coverage is improving but may be limited. Many students use mobile data (Dialog or Mobitel 4G are available in Oluvil). Consider a router for your room if stable internet is essential.

Q: What do I do if I have a problem with my roommate?
A: First, try to resolve the issue by politely communicating with your roommate. If unresolved, report it to the hostel Prefect (a senior student in a leadership role in the hostel) or directly to the Warden. The Warden has authority to mediate disputes and reassign rooms if necessary. Avoid escalating conflicts without trying the proper channels first.

Q: Are there off-campus rental options nearby?
A: Yes. Many boarding houses (known as 'boarding' in Sri Lanka) are available in Oluvil town and along the main road near campus. These are typically rooms rented from local families. Monthly rents range from LKR 3,000–8,000 depending on facilities. Ask senior students for recommendations — they know the best and safest options.

Q: What is the average monthly rent for a room near campus?
A: As of recent years, a basic room in Oluvil costs approximately LKR 3,000–6,000/month. Rooms with attached bathroom and internet may cost LKR 6,000–10,000/month. Prices are lower than Colombo. Sharing with another student reduces costs significantly. Budget separately for food, transport, and utilities.

Q: Can I cook my own food in the hostel?
A: Cooking inside hostel rooms is generally prohibited for fire safety reasons. Common kitchens may be available in some hostel blocks. Most students rely on the university canteen, nearby small restaurants (boutiques/kades), or negotiate with their boarding house landlady for home-cooked meals. A rice-and-curry meal at a local restaurant costs about LKR 150–300.

SECTION 6: FEES, FINANCE & SCHOLARSHIPS
==============================================================

Q: What fees does SEUSL charge for state undergraduate students?
A: As a state university student, your tuition is free — the Sri Lankan government funds undergraduate education. However, you are required to pay small administrative fees: registration fee, examination fees per semester, library fees, and hostel fees if you stay on campus. Total annual costs (excluding accommodation and living) are minimal compared to private institutions.

Q: Is university education free for Sri Lankan state university students?
A: Yes. Sri Lanka provides free university education to students admitted through the UGC system. This means no tuition fees for your undergraduate degree. This is a major benefit of entering a state university. However, living expenses, books, stationery, and personal costs are your responsibility.

Q: What is the Mahapola scholarship and how do I apply?
A: The Mahapola Higher Education Scholarship provides a monthly allowance to eligible undergraduates at state universities. Eligibility is based on academic merit and financial need (family income below a threshold). Apply through your faculty's student affairs office in the first month of registration. The allowance is approximately LKR 5,000–7,500/month and is paid directly to your bank account.

Q: What is the Bursary scholarship and who qualifies?
A: The Bursary is a needs-based financial grant from the university for students with proven financial hardship. It is separate from Mahapola and provides additional funds for living expenses, books, etc. Apply with an income declaration signed by your Grama Niladhari (GN officer) and other supporting documents. Both scholarships can sometimes be received simultaneously.

Q: How do I open a bank account to receive my scholarship?
A: Open a savings account at Bank of Ceylon (BoC) or People's Bank — these are the most commonly used banks for scholarship disbursements at SEUSL. Branches exist in Kalmunai (5 km from campus). Bring your NIC, admission letter, and index number. Mobile banking is available. Ensure your account number is correctly registered with the university scholarship office.

Q: What other financial aid or assistance is available?
A: Besides Mahapola and Bursary, look for: university welfare fund grants for emergencies, faculty-specific scholarships from donors, government social services support (if eligible), NGO scholarships for students from the Eastern Province, and corporate scholarships from companies that recruit from SEUSL. Check the notice boards and your faculty office regularly.

Q: What is the process to pay hostel or exam fees?
A: Pay hostel and examination fees at the Bursar's office cashier counter (cash) or via bank deposit to the university's designated account number (details from Bursar's office). Always obtain an official receipt — it is needed for clearance at the end of the year. Online payment options may be available; confirm with the Bursar's office.

Q: Are there part-time job opportunities on or near campus?
A: Part-time opportunities include: tutoring junior students, research assistant roles with faculty members, university library/admin assistant roles, and IT or typing work for faculty departments. In Kalmunai, some retail or service sector part-time work is possible. However, your primary focus should be studies — overworking part-time can harm your GPA.

Q: What is the cost of living per month in Oluvil?
A: Estimated monthly budget: Accommodation LKR 3,000–8,000 (if off-campus), food LKR 6,000–10,000 (canteen + restaurants), transport LKR 1,000–2,000, stationery/books LKR 500–2,000, personal LKR 1,000–3,000. Total: approximately LKR 12,000–25,000/month. Oluvil is one of the most affordable university towns in Sri Lanka.

Q: Are there any fee waivers for financially disadvantaged students?
A: Yes. Students with extreme financial hardship can apply to the Dean of Student Affairs for examination fee waivers or payment plans. The university also has an emergency welfare fund for sudden crises (illness, family bereavement). Apply with a letter explaining your situation and supporting evidence. The university generally tries to accommodate genuine hardship cases.

SECTION 7: CAMPUS FACILITIES & SERVICES
==============================================================

Q: Where is the main library and what are its opening hours?
A: The SEUSL main library (Dr. A.M.A. Azeez Library) is located at the center of the campus. Opening hours are typically Monday to Friday 8:00 AM – 8:00 PM, Saturday 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM. It is closed on Sundays and public holidays. Extended hours may be available before exams. The library has a large collection of books, journals, and digital resources.

Q: How do I register for library membership and borrow books?
A: Register at the library counter with your student ID card during the first week of university. You will receive a library membership card (or your student ID may serve this purpose). Typically you can borrow 2–4 books at a time for 2 weeks. Overdue books attract daily fines. Final-year students may have higher borrowing limits.

Q: Does SEUSL have a computer lab / IT centre?
A: Yes. Each faculty has dedicated computer laboratories, and there is a central IT centre. Labs are open during working hours for student use. Bookings may be required for specific project work. The Faculty of Technology has the most advanced lab facilities with specialized software.

Q: How do I access the university Wi-Fi network on campus?
A: University Wi-Fi (usually labelled SEUSL-WiFi or similar) is available in lecture halls, the library, and common areas. Connect using your student index number and a password provided by the IT division during registration (or from your department). If you have connection issues, visit the university IT support office.

Q: Is there a university cafeteria or canteen?
A: Yes. The main university canteen serves breakfast, lunch, and dinner on weekdays. Food is subsidized for students. Additionally, small student-run kiosks and privately operated food stalls operate near the hostel areas and faculty buildings. The main canteen serves Sri Lankan meals — rice and curry, short eats, and beverages.

Q: What sports facilities are available on campus?
A: SEUSL has: a cricket ground, football/soccer pitch, volleyball and netball courts, badminton courts, and a multi-purpose indoor sports hall. These are maintained by the Physical Education (PE) department. Inter-faculty tournaments and university-level competitions (SLUG Games) are held using these facilities.

Q: Is there a gym or fitness centre?
A: A basic gym or fitness facility may be available — check with the Physical Education department or student union for current availability and access procedures. Many students also exercise on the open sports grounds in the early morning or evening.

Q: Does SEUSL have a medical/health clinic on campus?
A: Yes, SEUSL has a university health centre staffed by a medical officer who holds regular clinical sessions. For minor ailments, medicines are provided free of charge. For serious conditions, students are referred to Kalmunai Base Hospital (approximately 5 km away). Keep the health centre contact number saved on your phone.

Q: Where is the student counseling or welfare office?
A: The Student Counseling and Welfare Unit operates under the Dean of Student Affairs. It provides confidential counseling for personal, academic, and emotional issues. It also handles welfare matters such as financial assistance applications. The office is in the main administration complex.

Q: Is there a mosque or prayer room on campus?
A: Yes. Given the predominantly Muslim environment of SEUSL and Oluvil, mosque facilities and prayer rooms are available on and near campus. Prayer times are respected in the academic schedule. Non-Muslim students are welcome in the environment and are treated with full respect.

Q: What is the student union and how does it work?
A: The Student Union is the elected body representing all students at SEUSL. It is led by a President, Secretary, and committee elected annually by student vote. The union organizes events (Freshers' Night, cultural shows, sports meets), advocates for student rights, and liaises with university management on student welfare issues. Getting involved is highly recommended.

Q: Are there ATMs or banks near the campus?
A: There are no ATMs directly on campus. The nearest bank branches and ATMs are in Kalmunai town (approximately 5 km from campus). Bank of Ceylon, People's Bank, and Commercial Bank have branches there. Plan your cash withdrawals — keep some emergency cash available on weekdays when transport is convenient.

Q: How do I use the university's Learning Management System (LMS)?
A: SEUSL uses an online LMS (often Moodle-based) accessible via the university website. Login credentials are your student index number and a password set during registration. The LMS is used to access lecture notes, assignment submissions, announcements, timetables, and results. Ensure your login works in the first week and check it daily.

Q: Is there a bookshop or stationery shop on campus?
A: A university bookshop or stationery counter may operate on campus — confirm with your faculty office. Most students purchase textbooks, stationery, and printing services from shops in Kalmunai. For specialized textbooks, online orders (from Vijitha Yapa or Sarasavi bookshops online) with delivery to Kalmunai is a practical option.

SECTION 8: STUDENT LIFE & EXTRACURRICULAR
==============================================================

Q: What student clubs and societies exist at SEUSL?
A: SEUSL has numerous student clubs including: Computer Society, Science Society, Management Society, Debating Club, Drama Society, Music Circle, Photography Club, Leo Club (community service), Muslim Students Association, Environment Club, and various faculty-specific societies. New clubs can be formed with faculty approval. Joining at least one society greatly enriches university life.

Q: How do I join a sports team or participate in university games?
A: Trials for university sports teams (cricket, football, volleyball, badminton, athletics, etc.) are held at the beginning of each academic year. Announcements are made on notice boards and via the student union. No prior professional experience is required for many teams — attitude and commitment matter. The Physical Education department coordinates all sports activities.

Q: Are there cultural events and festivals organized at SEUSL?
A: Yes. Major cultural events include: Freshers' Night (the big welcome event for first-year students), annual cultural shows and talent nights, Eid celebrations (respected by the whole university community), inter-faculty sports meets, photography and art exhibitions, and academic symposia. These are organized by the student union and faculty societies.

Q: What is Freshers' Night and how does it work?
A: Freshers' Night is the main celebratory event welcoming first-year students, organized by second-year students (seniors) a few weeks after orientation. It typically features cultural performances, music, welcome speeches, crowning of Freshers' King/Queen, and student skits. It is a memorable event — dress well and participate enthusiastically!

Q: Are there opportunities to participate in national university games (SLUG)?
A: Yes. SEUSL participates in Sri Lanka University Games (SLUG), the national inter-university sports championship. Top-performing athletes from university teams are selected to represent SEUSL. SLUG includes competitions in cricket, football, volleyball, athletics, swimming, and many other sports. Representing your university at SLUG is a prestigious achievement.

Q: How do I get involved in student government or the student union?
A: The student union holds annual elections — any registered student can contest for positions (President, Secretary, Faculty Representative, etc.). Even if you don't run for office, you can actively participate by attending general meetings, voting in elections, volunteering for events, and joining union sub-committees. Leadership roles in the union look excellent on your CV.

Q: Are there volunteer and community service programs?
A: Yes. The Leo Club (affiliated with Lions Club International) is the main community service organization at SEUSL. They organize blood donation campaigns, beach clean-ups, school tutoring programs in local communities, and disaster relief efforts. Volunteer participation is respected and valued by employers. Log your hours for your CV.

Q: What social activities happen in Oluvil outside campus?
A: Oluvil has the sea beach (just a short walk from campus) — a popular spot for students to relax in the evenings. The town has small restaurants, tea shops (kades), and markets. Day trips to Arugam Bay (world-famous surf beach, ~15 km away), Ampara town, Batticaloa, and national parks (Kumana/Lahugala) are popular student weekend outings.

Q: Are there opportunities for study abroad or exchange programs?
A: SEUSL has international partnerships with universities for exchange programs and joint research. Opportunities are limited but exist — check the International Affairs Division notice board. Additionally, programs like Erasmus+ (for exchanges with European universities) and scholarships from Japanese, Chinese, and Australian governments are occasionally available. Follow the notice board and university website.

Q: What international partnerships does SEUSL have?
A: SEUSL has MoUs (Memorandums of Understanding) with several universities in the region including institutions in India, Malaysia, and other countries. These enable student and staff exchanges, collaborative research, and dual degree opportunities. The International Relations office can provide a full list of current partners.

Q: Is there a drama or arts society?
A: Yes. SEUSL has an active Drama Society and Fine Arts circle. They produce theatrical performances in Tamil, Sinhala, and English for university events. Joining arts societies is a great way to build confidence, make friends across faculties, and participate in inter-university drama competitions.

Q: How do senior students typically help junior students settle in?
A: SEUSL has a strong culture of senior students mentoring juniors. Seniors help with: explaining course registration, sharing previous exam papers and notes, guiding you around campus and Oluvil town, introducing you to affordable boarding places, and providing emotional support in the first difficult months. The mentorship relationship is very important in Sri Lankan university culture.

SECTION 9: CAREER, INTERNSHIP & AFTER GRADUATION
==============================================================

Q: Does SEUSL have a career guidance or placement office?
A: Yes. SEUSL has a Career Guidance Unit (CGU) that helps students with career planning, CV writing, interview preparation, and job placement. They organize career fairs, company presentations on campus, and workshops. Visit the CGU office early in your degree — don't wait until final year.

Q: When does industrial training take place and how is it arranged?
A: Industrial Training (IT) typically takes place in Year 3 or between Year 3 and Year 4. Duration is usually 6 months to 1 year. The university provides a list of approved companies. You can also find your own placement (subject to university approval). Your HoD and the Career Guidance Unit coordinate placements. Start searching at least 3–4 months before the IT period begins.

Q: How do I find internship opportunities related to my degree?
A: Channels to find internships: SEUSL career guidance unit notice board, university website job postings, LinkedIn (set up your profile in Year 1), company websites, career fairs on campus, senior student networks, and faculty lecturers who have industry connections. The IT company list from your department is a good starting point.

Q: Does SEUSL have connections with companies for job placements?
A: Yes. SEUSL has partnerships with companies, particularly in IT, engineering, business, and public sector roles. The Faculty of Technology has the strongest industry links. Companies such as government ministries, IT firms, banks, and NGOs in the Eastern Province regularly recruit SEUSL graduates. The CGU maintains a database of recruiting employers.

Q: What is the average employment rate for SEUSL graduates?
A: SEUSL graduates have reasonable employment outcomes. Technology and Management graduates tend to find employment within 6–12 months of graduation. Government sector jobs, IT companies, banks, and NGOs are common employers. Building your skills (certifications, projects, internship experience) alongside your degree significantly improves employment outcomes.

Q: How do I obtain a certificate or letter of good standing for a job application?
A: Request a 'Letter of Good Standing' or 'Student Status Letter' from the Registrar's office. Submit your request in writing with your index number and the purpose stated. This is issued within 3–5 working days. For a reference letter, approach a lecturer or your Academic Advisor who knows your academic performance well — give them at least 2 weeks' notice.

Q: Can I do a postgraduate degree at SEUSL after my Bachelor's?
A: Yes. SEUSL offers MPhil, MSc, MBA, MA, and PhD programs. Eligibility is based on your undergraduate CGPA (usually 3.0+ for coursework master's, research experience for MPhil/PhD). Applications open once or twice a year. Check the Postgraduate Studies & Research Division (PGRD) at SEUSL for current programs and deadlines.

Q: Is there any alumni network I can join?
A: SEUSL has an active alumni association. Once you graduate, you can join and benefit from networking, job referrals, and mentoring juniors. During your studies, connecting with alumni on LinkedIn (search 'South Eastern University of Sri Lanka') can open doors to internships and job opportunities before you graduate.

Q: How do I get a certified copy of my degree after graduation?
A: After graduation, apply to the Registrar's office for certified copies of your degree certificate and academic transcript. Submit a written request, your NIC, and pay the applicable fee. Certified copies bear the university seal and Registrar's signature. Allow 1–2 weeks for processing.

Q: What careers typically come from each faculty at SEUSL?
A: Faculty of Technology: Software Engineer, IT Consultant, Network Engineer, Data Analyst. Faculty of Management & Commerce: Business Analyst, Accountant, Bank Officer, Entrepreneur. Faculty of Applied Sciences: Chemist, Biologist, Lab Technician. Faculty of Arts & Culture: Teacher, Translator, Public Servant. Faculty of Islamic Studies: Religious Scholar, Arabic Translator, Educational Administrator.

Q: Does the university help with CV writing or interview preparation?
A: Yes. The Career Guidance Unit (CGU) runs workshops on CV writing, LinkedIn profile building, and interview techniques. Faculty career societies also organize mock interview sessions with industry professionals. Take advantage of these from Year 1 — build your CV progressively rather than scrambling in final year.

Q: Are there entrepreneurship or startup support programs at SEUSL?
A: SEUSL encourages entrepreneurship, particularly through the Faculty of Management & Commerce and the Faculty of Technology. Student entrepreneurship competitions, business plan workshops, and links with incubators and startup ecosystems in Sri Lanka are available. The ICTA (ICT Agency of Sri Lanka) also runs programs accessible to university students.

Q: What are the requirements to graduate on time in 4 years?
A: To graduate in 4 years (8 semesters): complete all compulsory courses, complete the required number of credits (typically 120–130), maintain a minimum CGPA of 2.0, complete industrial training successfully, submit and defend your final year research project, clear all library and hostel dues, and obtain financial clearance from the Bursar's office. Starting strong in Year 1 makes all subsequent years easier.
