UNIVERSITY POLICIES - SOUTH EASTERN UNIVERSITY OF SRI LANKA
=============================================================

INSTITUTIONAL REPOSITORY (IR) POLICY
--------------------------------------
Reference: Library Committee Memo No: SEUSL/LC/21/52/05

The Institutional Repository of SEUSL (SEUIR) was established in 2006. It was initially built on GreenStone Digital Library (GSDL) software and later migrated to DSpace 4.0 in 2014, then upgraded to DSpace 5.5 in November 2015.

Website: http://ir.lib.seu.ac.lk/

MISSION:
The SEUIR is a nodal point of access for scholarship and creative work contributed by the university, with the goal of expanding the visibility of university intellectual scholarship to the regional and global community.

OBJECTIVES:
1. Collect and store institutional digital contents in a single platform
2. Provide open access to institutional research output
3. Archive, preserve, and disseminate institutional documents including unpublished literature

ACCEPTED CONTENT:
- Conference proceedings of SEUSL
- Thesis and Dissertations (undergraduate, postgraduate, and academic)
- Journals published by SEUSL
- Articles authored by SEUSL Academics (with copyright clearance where needed)
- Books and book chapters (non-textbooks) — case-by-case
- Data sets and/or descriptive metadata — case-by-case
- Grey literature (technical reports, research reports, working papers, annual reports, convocation addresses) — case-by-case

SUBMISSION RULES:
- Items shall only be submitted by members of SEUSL
- Only the SEUIR administrator or authorized person can deposit valid content
- Academics can upload articles to the "Research Articles" collection
- Authors are fully responsible for content validity and authenticity
- Plagiarized content is the sole responsibility of the author
- If the author does not own copyright, they must obtain publisher permission before submission
- Conference proceedings and journals should be sent as finalized documents
- University publications must be submitted within one calendar year

EXCLUSIONS:
- Confidential or administrative documents (reports, minutes)
- Research articles published in predatory journals or conferences
- Works with copyright issues
- Content not within coverage years of indexed databases

ACCESSIBILITY:
- Items are freely accessible via http://ir.lib.seu.ac.lk/
- Articles are indexed in Google and Google Scholar
- Creative Commons licenses are supported
- Authors retain full responsibility for copyright clearance

RETENTION:
- Scholarly materials are retained permanently unless they infringe copyright, contain falsified research, or fail legal requirements
- Items remain even if the depositor leaves SEUSL employment

WITHDRAWAL:
- Acceptable reasons: copyright violation, legal requirement, national security, falsified research, duplicates
- Authors must submit a prescribed form with editor/coordinator recommendation for withdrawal
- The sole authority over the Digital Library is with the Librarian

COPYRIGHT:
- Works comply with the University's Research Policy and Plagiarism Policy
- Authors retain full copyright when they own all rights
- When copyright is with a publisher, the author must obtain permission before repository submission

Responsible Executives: Vice-Chancellor, Deans of the Faculties, Librarian, SAL & Assistant Librarian
